# Product

## Register

product

## Users

SmartContract serves small and medium business teams that manage contracts across drafting, approval, legal review, finance, sealing, archiving, and fulfillment.

Primary users include ordinary employees drafting contracts, department leaders approving business terms, legal reviewers checking risk, finance staff tracking payment and delivery obligations, executives overseeing high-value agreements, and administrators managing roles and access.

They use the system inside a task-heavy workday, often while cross-checking documents, status, deadlines, approvals, and audit requirements. The interface should help them find the next action quickly without losing context.

## Product Purpose

SmartContract is a contract lifecycle management system powered by document intelligence and large language models.

It exists to make contract work safer and faster: create drafts, import or OCR source documents, fill template fields, save versions, review risks, submit approvals, register seals, archive final records, and monitor fulfillment milestones from one authenticated workspace.

Success means users can move a contract from draft to completion with clear status, traceable decisions, visible risks, and fewer manual handoffs.

## Brand Personality

Trustworthy, restrained, efficient.

The product should feel like a serious operational tool for sensitive business documents: calm enough for long sessions, structured enough for audit workflows, and confident enough that users trust the data and actions on screen.

The voice should be direct and practical. Labels and messages should describe what the user can do next, not market the feature.

## Anti-references

Do not make the product feel like a generic decorative SaaS landing page.

Avoid oversized hero treatments, gradient text, ornamental cards, excessive shadows, playful motion, novelty controls, and high-saturation color used as decoration.

Avoid layouts that hide operational density. Tables, filters, status tags, side navigation, and document editing controls should stay familiar, scannable, and consistent across pages.

Avoid vague empty states or error messages. Contract workflows need clear recovery paths and exact status language.

## Design Principles

Prioritize the next decision: every page should make the current contract state, risk, owner, and next action easy to identify.

Preserve trust through restraint: use color for status, selection, and primary actions rather than decoration.

Keep workflows continuous: drafting, template use, approval, risk review, archive, and fulfillment should feel like connected steps in one system.

Make auditability visible: important actions should leave clear records, timestamps, owners, and version context.

Design for dense work: support tables, filters, long documents, and side panels without visual noise or fragile responsive behavior.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA for text contrast, keyboard focus, form controls, and interactive states.

Respect reduced motion preferences. Motion should communicate state changes only and should not block task completion.

Use semantic controls where possible. Buttons, links, selects, inputs, dialogs, tables, and status labels should remain recognizable to assistive technologies.

Do not rely on color alone for status. Pair risk and workflow colors with labels, icons, or text.
